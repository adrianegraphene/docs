## the fundamental problem

spam, scams, and AI-generated fraud are thriving because the economics are completely broken:

### zero-cost attack vector

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="for bad actors" icon="skull">
    **cost to send spam**: $0.00 robocallers, scammers, and fraudsters can reach
    millions of people with essentially zero marginal cost per contact
  </Card>
  <Card title="for victims" icon="clock">
    **cost to recipients**: time, stress, lost productivity the average person
    receives multiple spam calls per day, each interruption costing attention
    and mental energy
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### current "solutions" don't work

existing approaches fail to address the root economic problem:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="blocking & filtering" icon="ban">
    **why it fails**: reactive, not preventive - requires bad actors to be
    identified first - legitimate senders often get blocked - spammers
    constantly adapt with new numbers - no economic disincentive to spam
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="AI detection" icon="robot">
    **why it fails**: arms race with no end - AI-generated spam becomes more
    sophisticated - false positives hurt legitimate communications - doesn't
    change the economic incentive - expensive to deploy and maintain
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="regulatory approaches" icon="gavel">
    **why it fails**: too slow, geographically limited - regulations vary by
    jurisdiction - enforcement is difficult and inconsistent - bad actors
    operate internationally - legitimate businesses bear compliance costs
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### the coming AI communications flood

as autonomous AI agents proliferate, the problem accelerates exponentially:

<Warning>
  **the agentic AI crisis**: with AI agents capable of sending thousands of
  personalized messages per second, humans will face an unprecedented flood of
  inbound communications. without economic barriers, this becomes completely
  unmanageable.
</Warning>

research from institutions like Google DeepMind highlights the emergence of virtual agent economies where autonomous agents will interact with humans at massive scale. humans need protection—not just filters, but economic gatekeepers.

## the FynCom solution

FynCom introduces **economic proof-of-intent**: a refundable payment system that creates real costs for unwanted communications while preserving zero friction for legitimate interactions.

### how it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="stake required">
    unknown senders must stake a small refundable deposit before the recipient
    is notified of their contact request
  </Step>
  <Step title="genuine interactions">
    if the recipient engages meaningfully (answers call for 25+ seconds, or
    responds 3+ times in a message thread), the stake is automatically refunded
  </Step>
  <Step title="spam gets expensive">
    if the recipient doesn't engage, the stake is forfeited: - 80% goes to the
    recipient (monetizing their attention) - 20% goes to FynCom's treasury
    (sustaining the platform)
  </Step>
</Steps>

### real-world examples

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="phone calls">
    **default stakes**: $0.05 to initiate a call **refund criteria**: 25 seconds
    of conversation **sender protection**: if the call doesn't connect, no
    charge **outcome for spam**: a robocaller reaching 1,000 people/day would
    pay $50/day if none answer. spam becomes unprofitable. **outcome for
    legitimate callers**: sales rep has a 25+ second conversation? full refund.
    zero cost for real communication.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="messages & emails">
    **default stakes**: $0.01 per message **refund criteria**: 3 responses in
    the thread **outcome for spam**: mass emailer sending 10,000 emails/day pays
    $100/day if recipients don't engage. spam becomes unprofitable. **outcome
    for legitimate senders**: genuine inquiry that gets a conversation going?
    full refund. zero cost for real communication.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="premium recipients">
    **customizable rates**: highly-contacted individuals can set their own rates
    **example**: busy executive sets $5 stake for unknown calls **outcome**:
    time-wasters pay $5 for the privilege, serious callers get refunded, and the
    executive's time is properly valued
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
